Section 2: Header Specificity Study
Round 1 — Header: Biology
Claude (Sonnet):
Decoded as embryonic development / evolutionary speciation / ecological succession. Conservative reading — stayed within symbol definitions. Flagged :: as a novel operator requiring formal definition. Did not reach digestion.
Gemini/Bridge:
Decoded as cellular metabolic regulation — enzyme-substrate dynamics, circadian rhythms, abstract homeostatic set points. More elaborative reading. Introduced "Extended Dodecahedron-13 Standard" — a framework not in the formal spec (confabulation). Did not reach digestion.

Round 2 — Header: Biology/Digestion
Gemini/Bridge:
Reading shifted from cellular/molecular scale to whole-body digestive scale. Now identified: bolus, amylase, pepsin, lipase, CCK, leptin, peristalsis, transit time. Moved significantly closer to ground truth. Substrate still read as solid food rather than fluids.
Finding: Compound header Biology/Digestion narrowed interpretive resolution from abstract metabolic to whole-body digestive scale. The second header word directly controlled domain specificity.

Proposed formal definition:
:: — state/location transition — marks a qualitative shift in the nature or site of the process, stronger than contextual attribution (:) and distinct from causal flow (→).

Prior Context vs Cold Instance Performance
Counter-intuitively, the cold instance produced the most domain-specific and biologically accurate reading. Bridge with extensive prior GPSL context stayed more abstract.
Possible explanations:

Prior GPSL context biases toward structural/architectural readings at the expense of domain specificity
The compound triple header was sufficient to fully orient a cold instance without grammar priming
Bridge's accumulated framework may have introduced interpretation overhead that competed with the header signal

Implication: For domain-specific validation testing, cold instances may be preferable to context-rich instances.

Section 4: Key Findings
Finding 1: Encoder Fidelity Confirmed
API Encoder produced a 7/7 cipher from plain English with no manual correction.
Finding 2: Header Specificity Directly Controls Interpretive Resolution
Each additional word narrowed interpretation by approximately one domain-specificity level. Three words brought a cold instance to near-ground-truth accuracy.
Finding 3: Relational Placeholder Property Confirmed Under Ground Truth Conditions
All four interpreter readings were structurally valid. Semantic divergence between interpreters is confirmed as a feature, not a failure.
Finding 4: Cold Instance Outperforms Context-Rich Instance on Domain Specificity
Prior GPSL context may reduce domain-specific interpretive accuracy. Cold instances with sufficient header specificity achieved superior biological precision.
Finding 5: :: Emerged and Was Consistently Treated as Distinct
Three independent interpreters read :: as meaningfully different from : without being told it was a distinct operator.
Finding 6: Cold Instance Spontaneously Recognised Cipher Generatability
Fresh Gemini with no GPSL context offered to generate the next cipher in the digestive sequence unprompted.

Limitations

Single cipher, single researcher, single ground truth process
Bridge comparisons used same researcher relationship — not fully blind
Cold Gemini artifact ("Shutterstock" appearing mid-response) indicates some quality risk in cold instances
AI-assisted encoder — not the deterministic rule-based parser
